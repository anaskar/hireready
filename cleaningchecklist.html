How to clean each room in the home

Conducting a detailed and thorough clean each and everytime will ensure you get more clients and stay active on the platform

Bathroom | Kitchen | Living Room | Bedroom | Floors | Detailed Clean



Cleaning the Bathroom

Getting Started in the Bathroomm
  • Remove everything from the bathroom including bath mats + trash cans and towels
  • Dust reachable vents , light fixtures , tops and corners of walls and cabinets
  • Spray down all surfaces with cleaning chemicals. Including the shower, toilet and sink area
  • Start wiping and cleaning the sink area, using glass cleaner on the mirror and medicine cabinets
  • Clean the toilet completely and make sure to remove all hair and dirt infront of and around the toilet
    • Never use the same rag that you used to clean the bathroom, anywhere else in the home
  • Scrub and rinse the shower
  • Replace all items back to where they were on the counter
  • Dry and make sure each surface feels and looks clean to the touch
  • Vacuum Bathroom rug before placing it back


Special Touches in the Bathroom

  • Shiny things must shine. Use glass cleaner to shine all stainless steel fixtures such as faucet, shower fixtures and doorknobs and handles
  • Fold toilet paper over and place a sticker to make it look neat and simple
  • Fold Towels into a tri-fold to hide all tags and edges
  • Place a toothbrush cover on the head of the clients toothbrushes
  • Leave toilet seat cover closed
  • Leave shower curtain opened revealing a clean, shiny shower
  • Push and roll toothpaste to the front of the tub

Cleaning the Kitchen

Getting Started in the Kitchen
  • First, Make sure you've collected dishes throughout the home
  • Work your way top to bottom
    1. Dust lights and tops of cabinets, as high as you can reach
    2. Wipe down cabinet faces and bring dirt down before doing countertops
  • Clean all appliances
    1. Clean refrigerator face and handles
    2. Spray chemical on stovetop and let sit while you clean backsplash, inside and outside the microwave
    3. Clean stove top and oven door and dry
    4. Small appliances should be moved to clean behind and under them. Clean out crumbs in toasters and coffeemakers
  • Dust and wipe furniture and baseboards


Special touches in the Kitchen
  • All Labels should face outwards and look organized
  • Wrap cords neatly for appliances
  • Place soap facing outwards on the right side of the kitchen faucet, place branded sponge on the left

Cleaning the Living Room

Getting Started in the Living Room
  • First, Make sure to remove dirty dishes if there are any
  • Work your way top to bottom
    1. Dust lights, tops of cabinets or walls
    2. Dust around windows
  • Clean all surfaces
    1. Dust and wipe down tables and shelves
    2. Use a dry, clean microfiber rag on electronics, including TV sets. No chemicals


Special Touches in the Living Room

  • Leave pillows fluffed , with a dent on top
  • Spray airfreshener
  • Fold all loose blankets and neatly stack items on tables

Cleaning the Bedroom

Getting Started in the Bedroom
  • Fold and stack neatly any lose clothing
  • Work your way top to bottom
    1. Dust light fixtures and around windows and walls
    2. Dust all electronics gently
    3. Dust photos and pictures gently
    4. Wipe down dressers, shelves and nightstands
  • Make the bed
  • Organize, tidy personal items, but do not move or misplace


Special touches in the Bedroom
  • Turn Down Service means after you make the bed neatly, leave the side closest to the door open

Cleaning Floors

Important details to remember when cleaning floors
  • Always check with the client if the floors appear to be Marble
  • Once you are sure the flooring can be mopped using multisurface chemicals, proceed to get started cleaning
  • First, get rid of all loose dirt by using a broom or vacuum
    • If carpet, vacuum in orderly zig zag lines to leave the marks on the carpet showing you've vacuumed thoroughly
    • For floors, vacuum under cabinets and corners in order to pickup any lose dirt to prepare the floors to be mopped
  • Second, start mopping using a swiffer with a microfiber rag
  • If using a Squirt and Mopproduct (such as Method)
    1. Wet your microfiber cloth and wring so it isn't dripping and soaking the floors
    2. Wrap it around the swiffer mop
    3. Squirt product onto the floor, and mop using the wet cloth
    4. Proceed to clean the area, check the cloth and if dirty, rinse in the sink and continue
  • Work your way from the corner of the room and mop your way out
    1. Don't forget hallways, entryways and stairs that require mopping
    2. Mop yourself out of the home to make sure you clean everything

Details to clean in all rooms





Important details to remember in all rooms
  • Always look for and clean HIGH HAND TRAFFIC areas
    1. Door knobs
    2. Light switches
    3. Door frames, bedroom and bathroom walls
    4. Cabinet doors
  • Work your way top to bottom
    1. Dust lights and around windows and walls
    2. Dust or vacuum baseboards and vents
  • Pay attention to presentation
    1. Organize, tidy personal items
    2. Arrange items with names facing out
  • Always leave with the trash
