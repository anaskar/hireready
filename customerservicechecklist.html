Customer Service

When working with clients it is important to provide great customer service

Preparation | Intro & Walk through | Finishing up



Being prepared for each appointment

Before each and every appointment
  • First, make sure you check the days prior to the appointment that you can make it
    • If you are unable to make an appointment you have to notify the company atleast 3 days in advance to find a replacement
    • Canceling last minute is unacceptable and can lead to decreased work hours
    • It is your responsibility to attend each appointment and be on time everytime
  • Check your supplies! Do you have everything you need to clean an entire home?
    1. Clean microfiber rags - do not bring used rags into a client home.
    2. All purpose/Multi Surface cleaner
    3. Bathroom cleaner
    4. Floor cleaner
    5. Scrub brushes and sponges
    6. Kitchen, 409 disinfectant cleaner
    7. Bathroom cleaner
    8. Paper Towels
    9. Vacuum cleaner
    10. Remember all of these supplies in order to completely clean each home
  • Plan your route to get the appointment on time
    • Being late is unacceptable
  • Put on a clean, branded shirt, so clients know who you are
  • Read the appointment notes before arriving to the home so you can know before meeting the client what their needs are
      They may ask for you to focus on specific thing or have additional requests such as inside of the fridge or oven. If this is the case you want to be aware so you can let them know when you meet them that you have already read the notes




Introducing yourself to a client

Proper Introduction
  • Remember to smile
  • Shake the client's hand
  • Make eye contact
  • Introduce yourself example: "Hi my name is ___. I am here to clean your home!"
  • "Thank you for having me today"
  • "Would you like me to take off my shoes?"

Depending on what you are comfortable with, adjust accordingly



Walk through

The walk through is an opportunity for you to show your professionalism to clients

  • This is extremely important if the client is home or not to walk through the entire home and know how long each rooom will take you so you can adjust
  • If the client is home politely ask him or her if they could briefly show you around
    1. "Would you please show me around?"
    2. "I see in your notes you said to focus on the bathroom, is there anything else you would like me to know to focus on before I get started?"


  • Ask questions such as:
    • "Is there anything you don't want me to touch today?""
    • "I didn't see a note on where to leave the trash, where can I take out the trash at the end of the appointment?"
    • "Do you have any special surfaces such as Marble that you would want me to use special products for?"
    • "I see you have papers here, is there anything you prefer me to leave alone?"
  • After the tour, show you are a professional and tell the client where you will get started
  • "I'm going to get started in the master bathroom unless you prefer me to get started somewhere else"

Finishing up!

Finishing up the appointment
  • First, check over your work by yourself
  • Grab a rag and some glass cleaner , incase you missed something or need to shine a surface, you'll want to be prepared
  • Go through each room of the home and check the following
    1. Are vents, lights, fans dusted?
    2. "High Hand Touch Surfaces" - Have you gotten all of them?
    3. How do the floors look? Make sure you didn't leave any hair in corners
  • Second, Check the details
    1. Towels - Folded neatly, no edges showing
    2. Toilet paper folded and neatly organized
    3. All products organized - Brand names facing outwards and arranged neatly


  • Lastly, walk through with the client
  • "Is there anything you see that I can do differently?"
  • This is where you point out something that you didn't know or couldn't do because a stain didn't come out, or because you were unsure whether something was clean or dirty
    • "I wasn't sure if the clothes were clean or dirty so I folded them and left them here"
    • "I washed the dishes and left them here to dry, if you could tell me where they go I will put them back before I leave"
    • "I tried to get this stain out but it didn't come out unfortunately"
  • Leave a note at the end and thank the client for having you
  • This is an opportunity for you to encourage them to have you again
    • "Thank you for having me, I hope to come back and clean for you again"
    • "Please let me know if there was anything you liked or didn't like so I can know for next time"
